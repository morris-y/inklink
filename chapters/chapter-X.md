He drinks in the smell of her, that dear familiar smell, the smell of childhood and long laughing afternoons. He murmurs into her neck *I love you, I love you* and kisses her timid, half open mouth like a man dying of thirst. 

He is dimly aware this is not how a prince ought to behave but she seems to welcome it, and it makes him want to weep that he can still feel anything other than pain. The push of her hips and the length of her legs is more intoxicating than all the jars of wine he has drunk, the softness and sweetness of her is almost enough to make him forget that Eurydice--no, don't think about Eurydice, here are bright smiling eyes and masses of warm chestnut-scented hair. He runs his fingers into it and holds her delicate skull, feeling, under his fingertips, the faint hammering of her heart.

The scent of pine needles on her breath makes him groan. Eurydice is dead but she is kissing his ear, Eurydice is dead but her forest-smell fills his mouth. He kisses her throat, her forehead, her chin, her cheeks. He places the palm of his hand flat against her stomach, knotting her dress in his fist, revealing her inner thighs to the night air. Maddened by the creamy smoothness of them he buries himself in her, plunges like sailor fleeing a burning ship, again and again, a rhythm as relentless and driving as Eurydice’s pink feet on polished stone. 

She whimpers as her fingers cradle his head, pressing his face into her neck. With a loud wail and great arch his longing for her for Eurydice for nothingness floods and finds release. She takes it with a gasp and as he collapses, trembling, wraps her arms around him tenderly, cradling him in her embrace. “I love you,” she says softly. “Orpheus, I‘ve always loved you.”

He says nothing and buries his face between her breasts. Hesypera presses gentle, ardent kisses on his hair. His hand drowsily finds hers and she holds it in gratitude. 

*Together*, she thinks, *we will face tomorrow, and all the days to come.*

He makes a sound that may be her name, or Eurydice’s, or both, and sleeps deeply for the first time in days.
